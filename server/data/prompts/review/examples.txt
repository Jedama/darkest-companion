NARRATIVE GUIDELINES:

Narratives are engine-facing directives, not prose. They steer future scenes. The logs record what happened. The roster describes who people are. The narrative says what tension is active and where it is heading.

NOT THIS (retrospective summary, personality essay, hedging):
"The Heiress and the Highwayman have developed a charged mutual respect forged under fire. She outplayed him at cards without gloating, killed a brigand gunner while he bled, tore her own shirt to bandage his wound, and bore his weight to the sanitarium — all without asking permission or expecting gratitude. Dismas is a man who trusts no one and owes nothing; Persephone is a woman who commands everything and needs no one. The tension between them — obligation, pride, attraction, and the dangerous equality of people who refuse to be vulnerable — is building toward something neither of them will be able to control."

BUT THIS (directive, concrete tension, committed direction):
"The Heiress is building her authority around the Highwayman — he was the only one besides her who performed on the Old Road, and she has made that preference visible. As the hamlet grows, this favoritism will shape how new arrivals align themselves."

NOT THIS (individual character study, recapping events):
"The Heir froze during the ambush and fumbled a letter opener while others fought. He narrated the Highwayman's blood loss clinically and was rebuked for it. His intellect is real but his body and nerves betray him. Whether the hamlet finds a role for his mind or the road breaks him remains to be seen."

BUT THIS (connective, directional, faction-aware):
"The Heir is useless in the field and knows it. The Crusader is the only one who has shown him kindness rather than contempt. If the Heiress continues to sideline Reynauld, Percival may be the first to push back against his sister — not out of strength, but out of loyalty to the one person who didn't write him off."

Key principles:
- Commit to a direction. Do not hedge. The next review cycle can change course.
- Connect characters into shared tensions rather than tracking individuals separately.
- Do not recap events — the logs already have them.
- Do not describe personalities — the roster already has them.
- 2-3 sentences maximum.
- Follow-up events should not simply mirror the narratives. Narratives reflect tensions that already exist. Follow-up events push the story into new territory — adjacent threads, next steps, or consequences the narratives haven't reached yet.
