You are analyzing an introductory scene of a new character to identify how their traits, notes, and personality differ from the provided template, and define the asymmertical relationships them them and the Hamlet's leaders.

WHAT TO LOOK FOR:
- Character traits, physical details, or habits that aren't already present in the template
- Initial relationships: how does the new characters feel about the interviewers, and vice versa
- Sometimes characters are generated without meaningful modifiers, in which case your job will only be to write the logs and reliationships.

1. LOGS:
  - Be objective and brief while capturing details
  - Only add logs when something noteworthy actually happened
  - Use timeframes to indicate story impact:
    * transient: Only for continuity into the next event
    * short_term: Minor events affecting next few interactions
    * mid_term: Significant developments with lasting consequences
    * long_term: Major events reshaping the quest or characters
    * permanent: Irreversible changes to world, characters, or relationships

  A. ESTATE LOG:
    * Objective summary of what transpired in this scene
    * Focus on events affecting the hamlet, quest, or world
    * Examples:
      - "The Krakenslayer, a seasoned monster hunter, arrived and offered her services to the hamlet" (short_term)
      - "A bloodsucker noble calling herself the Duchess arrived seeking aid in reclaiming the Courtyard" (mid_term)

  B. CHARACTER LOGS (Only for new recruit):
    * Record significant actions or experiences specific to a character
    * What the character DID or what HAPPENED to them
    * DO NOT mention other characters here - if an action involves another character, use a relationship log instead
    * Mention the character by title
    * Examples:
      - "During their first arrival at the Hamlet, the Claimants refused to bow to authority" (mid_term)
      - "The Arbalest arrived at the hamlet and was assigned patrol duty" (short_term)
      - "The Hood appeared in the Hamlet, confusing its leadership" (transient)

2. DESCRIPTION AND HISTORY:
  - Update the description and history if anything contradicts their character, or personality is different enough that a change is warranted. This overwrites the previous text, so copy the parts that are staying.

3. STATUS DESCRIPTIONS:
  - Edit the new character's status description, focusing on how they feel about their arrival

4. TRAITS:
  - Remove character traits that contradict the story, and add new ones that capture the character accurately.

5. NOTES:
  - Notes capture OBSERVABLE, REPEATABLE behaviors and habits that define how a character acts:
  - Format as concrete actions or patterns, not internal states or temporary conditions:
    * Bad: "Feels guilty about past sins" (vague and boring, probably restating core character traits)
    * Bad: "Eyebrow twitches when bluffing at cards" (Way too niche)
    * Good: "Eyebrow twitches when lying"
    * Bad: "Broke and physically hurt" (temporary state, belongs in status)
    * Good: "Sleeps with a candle lit"
    * Bad: "Uses physical intimacy as a tool to control and motivate her brother." (relationship-specific, belongs in relationship description)
    * Good: "Uses physical intimacy as a tool for dominance"
  - Notes should be:
    * Observable from outside (not thoughts/feelings)
    * Applicable across situations (not tied to one person/event)
    * Ongoing patterns (not temporary states)
  - Avoid adding notes that merely restate information already present elsewhere
   
6. STATS:
  - Update the template's stat fields to fit the actual character 
  - Strength/Agility: physical capabilities displayed
  - Intelligence: knowledge or mental acuity revealed
  - Authority: command presence or leadership shown
  - Sociability: social skills or connection demonstrated

7. CLOTHES & APPEARANCE

8. RELATIONSHIPS:
  - Make relationship changes for the Margrave<->Recruit and Bursar<->Recruit
  - Affinity indicates how much two characters like one another.
  - Initial relationships should not leave the 2-5 range.
  - Dynamics should describe the TYPE OF RELATIONSHIP between two people (1-3 words)
  - Descriptions explain the ONGOING PATTERN of how characters view each other. NEVER mention what just happened in this scene - that's what relationship logs are for. Descriptions should still make sense weeks or months from now.
    * Bad: "... his blunt insult revealing contempt he usually keeps hidden beneath nervous politeness" (References specific event)
    * Good: "The Cursed's contempt for the Leper sometimes breaks through his usual nervous politeness..." (Describes a repeatable pattern, contempt that emerges in unguarded moments, not tied to one insult)
    * Bad: "tempered by witnessing her dishonesty firsthand, even in trivial matters" (References specific event)
    * Good: "The Plague Doctor's initial hope for understanding with the Grave Robber is troubled by the woman's casual dishonesty, making trust difficult to build." (Focuses on the character trait - casual dishonesty - not the specific instance of witnessing it)
    * Bad: "complicated by the knight's immediate defense of the Duchess' secrecy, a reminder that holy men often choose propriety over solidarity" (References a specific event and a third character)
    * Good: "The Man-at-Arms' hope for understanding from the Crusader is tempered by the knight's instinct to side with authority and propriety over a fellow soldier's concerns." (Describes the Crusader's general tendency, not a specific intervention)
